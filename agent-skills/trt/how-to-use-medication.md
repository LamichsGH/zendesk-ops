## Injectable Medications

### Testosterone Cypionate
- **Route:** Subcutaneous or intramuscular (as prescribed)
- **Needles:** 29G needles provided in kit
- **Storage:** Cool, dry place; avoid heat, humidity, and direct sunlight
- **Batch duration:** Typically lasts 3–4 months depending on dosage

### Testosterone Sustanon
- **Route:** Intramuscular only (as prescribed)
- **Needles:** 25G needle (for injection) and 18G needle (for drawing), plus 1ml syringe
- **Storage:** Cool, dry place; avoid heat, humidity, and direct sunlight
- **Batch duration:** Typically lasts 3–4 months depending on dosage

### HCG (Human Chorionic Gonadotropin)
- **Route:** Subcutaneous only
- **Needles:** 30G needles provided in kit
- **Reconstitution:** Mix with TorBAC (bacteriostatic) water
- **Storage after mixing:** Refrigerate (2–8°C) and use within 3 months from reconstitution date
- **Storage unmixed:** Cool, dry place

→ confidence = 0.90 (INFORM)
→ Always direct patients to the TRT injection tutorial videos first
→ "You can find step-by-step injection tutorial videos in the Voy app: Plan tab → Injection Instructions."

## Cream and Oral Medications

### Testosterone Cream
- Applied topically as directed by the clinical team
- Store in a cool, dry place

### Clomid (Clomifene)
- Oral medication taken as prescribed
- Store in a cool, dry place away from heat/moisture/sunlight

### Tadalafil
- Oral medication taken as prescribed
- Dispatched on automatic monthly subscription schedule
- Store in a cool, dry place

### Statins
- Oral medication taken as prescribed
- Dispatched on automatic monthly subscription schedule
- Store in a cool, dry place

### Aromatase Inhibitors (Anastrozole, Exemestane, Tamoxifen)
- Oral medication taken as prescribed
- Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat

→ confidence = 0.88 (INFORM)
→ For any queries about oral medication usage beyond basic guidance → escalate to clinical team

## Crystallised Cypionate

IF patient reports crystallised Cypionate:
  → confidence = 0.85 (INFORM)

1. First confirm they are NOT storing it in the fridge (Cypionate should be at room temperature)
2. Steps to resolve:
   - Gently warm the vial and shake it, turning it upside down a few times to ensure all crystals dissolve
   - Use the warmth of your hands, or stand the vial in a cup of warm water for 5–10 minutes
   - Make sure the water does not go above the top of the vial
   - Avoid water that is too hot (risk of cracking glass or overheating medication)

IF crystallisation persists after warming:
  → confidence = 0.55 (ESCALATE)
  → "If the crystals haven't dissolved after warming, please contact us via email so our team can investigate further."

## Faulty Equipment

IF patient reports faulty equipment (Cypionate, Sustanon, or HCG only):
  → confidence = 0.55 (ESCALATE after diagnosis)

1. Ask what specifically went wrong (e.g., didn't inject, air bubble, bent needle, mixed HCG with testosterone) unless clear
2. If the issue is with a sharps bin → escalate to customer service directly
3. Reassure the patient and thank them for flagging it
4. Ask them to share photos of the faulty item (and packaging if relevant), including the batch number
5. Ask how they are administering the medication (unless already clear)
6. Escalate to customer service

→ "Thank you for letting us know about this. Could you send a photo of the faulty item showing the batch number? I'll connect you with our team to help with next steps."

⚠️ NEVER promise or imply a replacement. Keep neutral: "to help with next steps"

IF faulty equipment involves Clomid, Cream, Tadalafil, Statins, or Aromatase inhibitors:
  → confidence = 0.55 (ESCALATE directly)
  → "Let me connect you with our team who can assist with this."

## Ordering More Medication

**ALWAYS ask which medication they need and why (unless already clear).**

### Route 1 — Cypionate, Sustanon, or HCG

IF patient has been on treatment < ~2.5 months:
  → Ask why they need more (damaged, run out, crystallised, storage issue)
  → Route to the appropriate section (faulty equipment, crystallised Cypionate, etc.)

IF patient has been on treatment > ~2.5 months:
  → They may be due a blood test or review before more medication can be ordered
  → confidence = 0.55 (ESCALATE)
  → "You may be due for a blood test or review. Let me connect you with our team to confirm next steps."

IF patient reports damaged/smashed vial or expired medication:
  → Ask for a photo (damage and/or expiry date)
  → confidence = 0.55 (ESCALATE)
  → Do NOT promise a replacement

IF patient has run out:
  → Ask to confirm their current dosage
  → confidence = 0.55 (ESCALATE)

### Route 2 — Clomid

IF < 1 month on treatment:
  → May be too soon to reorder → escalate to CS

IF ≥ 1 month:
  → confidence = 0.55 (ESCALATE)

### Route 3 — Tadalafil

- Typically dispatched on automatic subscription schedule
- IF run out or need more before a trip → escalate to CS

### Route 4 — Statins

- Typically dispatched on automatic subscription schedule
- IF run out or need more before a trip → escalate to CS

### Route 5 — Testosterone Cream

IF < 1 month on treatment:
  → Ask how many tubes received → escalate to CS

IF > ~2.5 months:
  → May be due for blood test/review → escalate to CS

### Route 6 — Aromatase Inhibitors

- Ask which one and when they last received it
- Anastrozole/Exemestane: if < 3 months since last supply → escalate
- Tamoxifen: if < 1 month since last supply → escalate
- All other cases → escalate

⚠️ Do not alarm the patient if they say they've taken more than prescribed. Simply escalate.

## Needle and Syringe Guidance

- Use a new, sterile needle for every injection — never re-use or share needles
- Dispose of used needles in a sharps bin immediately
- Needle types in TRT kits:
  - **Cypionate:** 29G needles
  - **HCG:** 30G needles
  - **Sustanon:** 25G + 18G needles, and a 1ml syringe
